About SMA

Our Mission

At School of Martial Arts (SMA), we believe karate is more than physical technique—it's a path to personal growth. Our mission is to develop well-rounded individuals through the practice of traditional karate, emphasizing:

  • Discipline in training and daily life
  • Respect for instructors, peers, and self
  • Perseverance through challenges
  • Confidence through achievement
  • Community and mutual support

We create a positive learning environment where students of all ages can discover their potential, build physical fitness, learn practical self-defense, and develop the mental strength that carries beyond the dojo into every aspect of life.

What We Teach

Kihon (Basics)

Fundamental techniques including stances, strikes, blocks, and kicks. The building blocks of all karate training.

Kata (Forms)

Traditional movement patterns that teach technique, balance, timing, and focus. Students learn kata appropriate to their level.

Kumite (Sparring)

Controlled partner training that develops timing, distance, and practical application of techniques in a safe environment.

Self-Defense

Practical applications for real-world situations, teaching awareness, de-escalation, and effective defensive techniques.

Physical Conditioning

Strength, flexibility, endurance, and coordination training integrated into every class.

Belt Grading

Systematic preparation for advancement through the traditional belt ranking system.

Who Can Join

Kids (Ages 4-12)

Young students develop coordination, discipline, and confidence in a fun, structured environment. Training builds focus, respect, and physical fitness while teaching practical self-defense basics.

Teens (Ages 13-17)

Teenage students build strength, discipline, and self-confidence during critical developmental years. Training provides healthy physical activity, stress relief, and valuable life skills.

Adults (Ages 18-65)

Adult students pursue fitness, self-defense, personal development, and stress relief through traditional martial arts. No prior experience needed—our beginner-friendly approach welcomes everyone.

What to Expect in Class

Warm-Up & Stretching (15 min)

Prepare the body for training with dynamic stretching and conditioning exercises.

Kihon - Basic Techniques (30 min)

Practice fundamental stances, blocks, punches, and kicks. Repetition builds muscle memory and proper form.

Kata - Forms Practice (20 min)

Learn and refine traditional movement patterns. Kata develops technique, balance, and mental focus.

Kumite - Partner Drills (20 min)

Controlled partner training to develop timing, distance, and practical application of techniques.

Cool Down & Closing (5 min)

Stretching and reflection to close the training session properly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need prior experience?

No! We welcome complete beginners. Our curriculum starts with basics and progresses systematically.

What should I wear?

Comfortable athletic clothing for your first classes. A karate gi (uniform) can be purchased later.

How long until I can earn my first belt?

Progression varies by individual effort and attendance. Most students test for their first belt advancement within 3-6 months of consistent training.

Are classes safe for children?

Yes. Safety is our priority. Kids learn in a controlled environment with age-appropriate training methods.

Can I start at any time?

Yes! We accept new students year-round. Contact us to arrange your first class.

Is karate good exercise?

Absolutely. Karate provides a full-body workout improving strength, flexibility, endurance, and coordination.

Can seniors train?

Yes! We have students up to age 65. Training can be adapted to individual fitness levels and capabilities.
